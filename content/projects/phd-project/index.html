This PhD project explores the development of a system for safe and efficient human-robot collaboration (HRC) within industrial assembly lines. By utilizing advanced technologies such as 3D sensors, wearables, and elastic joint robots, the research aims to enhance the interaction and collaboration between human operators and robots.

Objectives
  • Utilize sensor technology to detect, track, and predict human motion.
  • Integrate tactile feedback and augmented reality (AR) for intuitive HRC.
  • Ensure safe HRC through the integration of intrinsically safe robots and intelligent systems.
Research Questions
  • How can 3D sensory information be used to accurately detect and predict human motion in real-time industrial environments?
  • How can tactile feedback and AR be integrated to facilitate intuitive and efficient HRT in demanufacturing tasks involving used goods?
  • How can we efficiently and safely perform HRT by integrating safe robots, tactile and AR interfaces with human detection and motion prediction?
